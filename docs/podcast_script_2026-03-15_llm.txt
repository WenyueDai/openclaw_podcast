These three papers together suggest a broader theme: we've been making simplifying assumptions about what information matters in biological systems, and those assumptions are often wrong. Whether it's assuming group averages capture individual variation, assuming spatial morphology is necessary for component identification, or assuming colormap choice is arbitrary — each of these papers shows that the details we've been glossing over actually contain crucial information for understanding and manipulating biological systems.
